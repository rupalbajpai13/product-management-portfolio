===========================================
AI PRODUCT MANAGEMENT PLAYBOOK
===========================================

1. 5 Whys
Purpose:
Find the real root cause instead of solving symptoms.

Used For:
- Problem Discovery
- Root Cause Analysis

-------------------------------------------

2. Jobs To Be Done (JTBD)
Purpose:
Understand what job the customer is hiring the product to perform.

Used For:
- Product Discovery
- Feature Design
- Customer Understanding

-------------------------------------------

3. User Journey Mapping
Purpose:
Visualize the current user workflow, pain points, and opportunities.

Used For:
- UX Design
- Process Improvement
- AI Opportunity Identification

-------------------------------------------

4. Opportunity Solution Tree (OST)
Purpose:
Connect business outcomes to opportunities, solutions, and experiments.

Used For:
- Feature Discovery
- Product Strategy
- Prioritization

-------------------------------------------

5. Product Discovery
Purpose:
Validate the problem before building the solution.

Steps:
Problem → User Research → Hypothesis → Prototype → Validation

-------------------------------------------

6. Lean Canvas
Purpose:
Create a one-page business model for a product idea.

Covers:
- Problem
- Solution
- Users
- Value Proposition
- Channels
- Revenue
- Cost

-------------------------------------------

7. Double Diamond
Purpose:
Structured design thinking framework.

Stages:
Discover
Define
Develop
Deliver

-------------------------------------------

8. Kano Model
Purpose:
Categorize features based on customer satisfaction.

Categories:
- Must Have
- Performance
- Delighters

-------------------------------------------

9. RICE Prioritization
Purpose:
Prioritize product backlog.

Formula:
Reach × Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort

-------------------------------------------

10. MoSCoW Prioritization
Purpose:
Define MVP scope.

Categories:
- Must Have
- Should Have
- Could Have
- Won't Have

-------------------------------------------

11. North Star Metric
Purpose:
Define the primary metric representing customer value.

Examples:
- Investigation Time
- Successful Investigations
- Recommendation Acceptance

-------------------------------------------

12. HEART Framework (Google)
Purpose:
Measure user experience.

Metrics:
- Happiness
- Engagement
- Adoption
- Retention
- Task Success

-------------------------------------------

13. AARRR Metrics (Pirate Metrics)
Purpose:
Measure product growth.

Stages:
- Acquisition
- Activation
- Retention
- Referral
- Revenue

-------------------------------------------

14. Build → Measure → Learn
Purpose:
Continuous product improvement.

Cycle:
Idea → Build → Measure → Learn

-------------------------------------------

15. Design Thinking
Purpose:
Human-centered problem solving.

Stages:
Empathize
Define
Ideate
Prototype
Test

-------------------------------------------

16. Product-Market Fit (PMF)
Purpose:
Validate whether the product solves a real market need.

Questions:
- Do users love it?
- Would users miss it?
- Are they willing to pay?

-------------------------------------------

17. OKRs (Objectives & Key Results)
Purpose:
Define measurable business goals.

Structure:
Objective
Key Results

-------------------------------------------

18. Outcome vs Output
Purpose:
Focus on customer value rather than feature count.

Output:
Built 10 features.

Outcome:
Reduced investigation time by 40%.

-------------------------------------------

19. AI Product Evaluation
Purpose:
Measure AI quality.

Metrics:
- Accuracy
- Grounding
- Hallucination Rate
- Recommendation Quality
- Confidence Calibration
- Explainability

-------------------------------------------

20. Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)
Purpose:
Keep humans involved in important AI decisions.

Used For:
- Approval workflows
- Enterprise AI
- High-risk decisions

-------------------------------------------

21. Responsible AI
Purpose:
Build trustworthy AI systems.

Principles:
- Fairness
- Transparency
- Explainability
- Privacy
- Accountability

-------------------------------------------

22. RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation)
Purpose:
Ground LLM responses using enterprise knowledge.

Pipeline:
Retrieve → Augment → Generate

-------------------------------------------

23. Agentic AI
Purpose:
AI autonomously plans and executes tasks.

Concepts:
- Planner
- Tool Calling
- Memory
- Multi-Agent Collaboration

-------------------------------------------

24. ReAct (Reason + Act)
Purpose:
AI reasons, performs actions, observes results, and continues iteratively.

Cycle:
Thought → Action → Observation → Repeat

-------------------------------------------

25. Architecture Decision Records (ADR)
Purpose:
Document important architectural decisions.

Structure:
- Context
- Decision
- Consequences

-------------------------------------------

26. Product Metrics
Purpose:
Measure product success.

Examples:
- User Adoption
- Time Saved
- Accuracy
- User Satisfaction
- AI Acceptance Rate

-------------------------------------------

27. AI Evaluation vs User Feedback
Purpose:
Understand the difference.

AI Evaluation:
Measures technical quality automatically.

User Feedback:
Measures customer satisfaction and usefulness.

-------------------------------------------

28. Explainable AI (XAI)
Purpose:
Help users understand why AI made a recommendation.

Examples:
- Evidence
- Rules Applied
- Confidence
- Supporting Knowledge

-------------------------------------------

29. Platform Thinking
Purpose:
Build reusable capabilities instead of one-off features.

Example:
Invoice Investigation today.
Enterprise Investigation Platform tomorrow.

-------------------------------------------

30. Product Vision & Roadmapping
Purpose:
Define long-term product direction.

Artifacts:
- Vision
- Roadmap
- Product Principles
- Success Metrics

===========================================
